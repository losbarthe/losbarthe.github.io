Google Earth Engine - NDVI Map

This simple interactive map, created using Google Earth Engine, focuses on the Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI) in Central New Zealand. NDVI is a crucial metric for understanding vegetation health, vigor, and density, providing insights into environmental conditions and land use practices. Navigate the map by zooming in on specific areas and toggle the settings of the background map.

About NDVI:

The Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI) is a widely-used remote sensing index that measures and monitors plant health and density using satellite imagery. It calculates the difference between near-infrared (which vegetation strongly reflects) and red light (which vegetation absorbs) to determine the presence and condition of vegetation.

If the web app does not work, find it externally here.

When looking at the map the main contrasting regoin is the mountain range. Here we can see a relatively low NDVI value as the mountains are probably too high for much vegetation to grow. Another interesting area is Christchurch (southwest in our region of interest). This, being one of New-Zealands main cities on the southern isand, has a surprisingly high NDVI value.

Creating the Map:

To create this map remote sensing NDVI data was uploaded into Google Earth Engine. Additionally, a polygon shapefile of the region of interest was made and uploaded. After this a simple script to generate the NDVI imagery was coded in JavaScript. Here, the data was filtered to select non-cloudy, summer imagery to best capture the NDVI potential of the region. Adding the script for a legend made this simple interactive map ready to be pblished.

Possible extentions of this projects could be to add another vegitation index for comparison or allowing the user to manually adjust the temporal range. For the purposes of this project I have kept the app in its simple form.
